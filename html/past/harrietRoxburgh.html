Casting Spells
adjective


1. A state of being affected by alcohol and/or a medicine or other substance which has a physiological effect when ingested or otherwise introduced into the body to the extent of losing control of one's faculties or behavior, notably when the person can no longer communicate effectively, and speech begins to resemble the act of speaking in tongues.

Casting Spells is Harriet Roxburgh's first solo exhibition since graduating with a Master of Contemporary Art from the Victorian College of the Arts.

Harriet Roxburgh (b. 1993, Sydney, Australia) is a Melbourne based artist who primarily works with paint to explore how contemporary social environments can be depicted and documented in the historical practice of painting.

The works take inspiration from her surroundings, mainly her social interactions and environments. The subject matter is often an interpretation made from acute observations of shared experiences and histories. Traditional Western subject matter is reinterpreted through the lens of contemporary youth culture. An outspoken character and lifestyle is depicted - which seldom conveyed though this fine art medium, as it is often a reserved topic for a niche sector of society.

Roxburgh has exhibited in array of group shows in both Sydney and Melbourne. She has completed a Bachelor of Visual Arts from the Sydney College of the Arts in 2014 and she graduated with a Master of Contemporary Art from the VCA in 2017.
