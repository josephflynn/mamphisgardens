REMOTE INVASION

Nick Millen

25th May - 3rd June

Download the Catalogue


There are the paintings I am making and there are the paintings that make themselves.

In so much as these two types of paintings are different expressions of the same action, they represent for me conscious and unconscious process and hence, they belong together in order to create whole pictures.

Their assemblage/coming together nonetheless presents problems as, initially, they may feel incongruent.

The work has been a chance to imagine and consider potentials of peace and balance between opposing forces.

Crib

Millen is an avid painter with a background/continued interest in performance and sculpture. His paintings are diverse, energetic, highly colourful and conceptual in nature; addressing his unrelenting intrigue into human consciousness, unconsciousness, desire, dreams and modern and ancient sociology.

‘My paintings are internal rhythms becoming form...
‘How we think and/or feel inhabits the gestures we make. Painting lays these internalisations bare because, perhaps unwittingly, painting is always an action; a dance, a stroke, a swipe, or a click – regardless, some movement must be enacted/played out and the paint traps your movement in time and reflects you, or your lie. Building up layers of paint creates a visual diary where you can observe a litter of moods, emotions, ideas and risks that gradually merge to make a whole – an anti- narrative. Seeking/divining that wholeness is the spinal cord of my process...

‘In other words, painting helps me be a little more self-aware and has (to date) kept me out of the doctor's office’.
Millen has exhibited widely over the past 8 years in Melbourne, Sydney, Brisbane, Gold Coast and Tasmania. He has also exhibited internationally during this time in Japan, Taiwan, Macau and New York. He is soon to take residency in France for two months.
Millen directed both Egg Gallery and House of Bricks Gallery and Studios in Melbourne (2011-2016), hosting over a hundred exhibitions from local and international artists.

You are invited to join us for his latest instalment, REMOTE INVASION – a fun exploration in to the openly direct, personal and clandestine forces shaping modern behaviour and painting.
‘I am less worried about robots and more worried about becoming one myself. Sometimes I feel like someone is performing brain surgery on me at night from some distant place. I tell them to stop but they don't.’ –NM
